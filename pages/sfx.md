 I read, and have read, a LOT of manga over the years. It's one of the driving interests that led me to compete in Japan Bowl in high school, pursue translation as a hobby, and (currently) explore manga, sound effects, and the Japanese language as field of research.

As a side-effect of reading so much manga, I get to encounter lots of new, interesting sound effects. The sound effects in Japanese manga are often broken into four different categories of sound symbolism:
- _giseigo_ or 擬声語 are onomatopoeia that mimic the sounds of living things
- _giongo_ or 擬音語 are onomatopoeia that mimic the sounds of inanimate objects
- _gitaigo_ or 擬態語 are mimetic words that describe a physical form or state
- _giyouogo_ or 擬容語 are mimetic words that describe movements and motions.
- _gijougo_ or 擬情語 are mimetic words that describe psychological states or feelings

### Interesting Sound Effects

ゴウンゴウン (goun goun)
- giongo
- This sound effect describes the low rumbling of a motor or large machinery, and is often translated as *grumble*, *rumble*, or *clunk*
I saw this sound effect recently while reading through a new series, See you at the Laundromat tonight by Kennoji and Rurie.
