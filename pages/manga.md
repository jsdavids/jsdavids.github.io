I read, and have read, a LOT of manga over the years. It's one of the driving interests that led me to compete in Japan Bowl in high school, pursue translation as a hobby, and (currently) explore manga, sound effects, and the Japanese language as field of research.

Current reading:
- Tsubame Tip-Off! by Watanuki Hiroya
A high school basketball manga with some girl's love themes. The art is nice, and I appreciate that each of the key characters have very distinct visual designs that stand out on the court. I almost (almost) want to start playing basketball because of this manga, it does a great job of illustrating the struggles of picking up the sport and catching up to more skilled teammates.
- Dig It by Yoshida Maru
Another high school sports manga, though this one focuses on boy's volleyball. Although the art style looks a little on flat and cartoony side, I've found that it has its own brand of charm. I just enjoy reading about the main character unlocking and honing volleyball skills, and seeing the characters try their best ignites that same sort of excitement as watching a close ball game in me.
- Idolatry by Shin Ootaka and Homare

Other recommendations:
- Chainsaw Man by Fujimoto Tatsuki
It's hard for me to overstate how much I enjoyed reading part 1 of Chainsaw Man. It's an action manga that focuses on Denji, an orphan who contracts with the Chainsaw devil to slay evil, and features some great fight scenes. To me though, the compelling parts of the story involve Denji's personal growth as he struggles with what it means to be human and how to form bonds with those around him. 
- Detective Conan by Aoyama Gosho
With over 1000 chapters and counting, Detective Conan (also known as Meitantei Conan or Case Closed!) can be daunting to start, but good mystery stories with well explained motives and hidden mechanisms are hard to come by. (I may also just be too heavily invested in the characters and the overarching story at this point, having read up to the most recent chapter)
- Wotakoi: Love Is Hard for Otaku by Fujita
I just relate heavy with this one. Wotakoi is a romantic comedy that focuses on a couple who hides their relationship from their coworkers on the grounds that they also need to hide their associations to nerdom.
